Tealight Rising.

She arrived from the garden, so pure, she
absorbed and handed out dazzling beams.
The tealight seeks to be honorous, like the
rays. In the cavern the solid wax, topped
with molten manna, levitates, radiating through the
fine vessel. She lifts her endured upright
thread, carrying the flame, firmly. Sturdily. The
tealight seeks purity, yet she pushes to consume, filter, and
drain the abyss. Repeat. Flickering prayers, though most are
fleeting, opening passages, though all are too
tapered, all she yearns is to saturate the petals until they are
star ruby. The afloat tealight seeks to provide incense for the
welcoming ritual. But raven smoke rises sinking the ritual,
prematurely. The incense is burnt, sunken. Flickering, no longer.
Ash rises aboves, still sheltering hubris.



Harvested petals, floating.

Before the amber warmth, he floated with his loose
comrades. Change, they detest. But their veins
were still fresh from before the forced harvest.
The rose petals still sail with only pure pride.
Still fragile, balancing on the forbidding static
loch, his mind rests with others, content with void,
blindness. More fervent and forbearing,
frequent flares approach, so he becomes
a rider of wrangled doubt, and he suspects
faith, and so he points: the wine glass is becoming crimson
Questioning luster, it is carnage he expects.
They are not used to being surrounded by loving fragrance.

So unnerved, they went, not expecting tranquility;
they returned to the past, again.
